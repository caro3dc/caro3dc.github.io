Stream Summary

The stream was an informal and improvised podcast, wherein the hosts asked themselves questions about various topics, such as fruits, career plans, personal feelings and experiences, as well as questions that the audience submitted to them.

The stream attracted a substantial number of viewers, including two teachers.

Things We Did Well

The feedback that we received from our feedback form noted that our podcast was humorous and interesting to watch, and that the topics were handled well. They also noted that the conversations sounded authentic and not scripted.

This success can be attributed to a lack of commitment to formality, both in the actions and words of the hosts, and in the nature of the questions that were being asked.

Furthermore, the plans for the podcast stream were intentionally non-solid, so that we, the hosts, could wield creative and conversational liberties than what would be possible in a practiced and scripted stream.

Things We Did Not Do Well

Bad Audio Quality

We have agreed among each other that we have to improve the quality of our recording devices, as we faced some technical difficulties (especially regarding our audio quality) during the duration of the stream.

Unfortunately, improving on this requires buying better equipment, which in turn requires spending money that we do not have.

Things We Did Not Do Well

Unequal Participation

One person from our audience also stated that some hosts were not participating as much as others, and we do agree with them and concede that that is something that needs to be improved upon.

We think that the reason for why this was the case is partly personality ‚Äì some were less keen to take initiative to answer questions than others ‚Äì and partly because some of the hosts did not call on others as much as some.

Audience Engagement

Our audience engagement was on an unexpected level ‚Äì the audience kept on asking questions during the podcast to the point that the hosts could almost not keep up, which helped the event be more lively and interactive.

According to our internal data, we had 36 unique viewers and 16 unique chatters, which shows that many of our audience were willing to engage with our stream.

Audience Engagement

Some of our viewers even had the initiative to directly participate in the conversation, answering in the live chat the questions the hosts were asking themselves.

At the start of the stream, only some of the audience were able to chat, owing to low initial viewership ‚Äì though as the stream progressed, the audience increased in size, peaking at 19 viewers and including two of our teachers.

As to why this level of engagement was the case, we share sentiments with our viewers, who said in the feedback survey that the topics were handled well, and that our conversations and humour were authentic enough to want to engage with.

Furthermore, our stream happened one week after the streams of some of the other groups in A-10-4, which meant that more people had the time to join and engage with our stream.
